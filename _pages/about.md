I am a PhD student in Machine Learning at Georgia Tech, based in the [H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering](https://www.isye.gatech.edu/), advised by Prof. Gian-Gabriel Garcia. My research sits at the intersection of **decision-focused learning**, **sequential decision-making under uncertainty**, and **reinforcement learning**, with a focus on healthcare applications where decisions are shaped by limited resources and noisy observations.

Current projects include: learning neural index policies for restless multi-action bandits with heterogeneous budgets (with applications to clinical deterioration in hospitalized children), resource-constrained diagnostic test design for concussion assessment, and studying the limits of vision-language LLMs for physical reasoning.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as an Applied Scientist at [Lucas Systems](https://www.lucasware.com/) (2019–2022) building optimization and ML tools for warehouse operations, and was an Applied Scientist Intern at AWS in 2025 designing AWS's first time-series forecasting framework for ML traffic between EC2 and S3 GPU fabrics. As an undergraduate, I was an AI research scholar at MIT and CERN, working on deep learning models for track reconstruction at the Large Hadron Collider beauty experiment.

I received my B.S. in Computer Science (with minors in Mathematics and Physics) from the University of Cincinnati in 2019, where I was a University Honors Scholar.
